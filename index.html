Research Interests

Massive Stars, Python & Observational Astronomy

My research encompasses core-collapse supernovae host sites in the Fireworks Galaxy with Prof. Emily Levesque, Milky Way open cluster metallicity with Prof. Ana Larson, OB stars within the LMC with Prof. Philip Massey, and the intergalactic medium using Hubble COS spectra with Prof. Jessica Werk.

Studying Supernovae Environments in the Fireworks Galaxy

Prof. Emily Levesque and my project ultimately aims to determine what makes the progenitor environments of galaxies so prone to high CCSN production. Using slit spectra from the APO 3.5m DIS instrument, our 52 different slit positions have yielded metallicity profiles at more than 100 different sites across the galaxy. Play with NGC6946's observed metallicity profile in 3D here.

Supported by a Mary Gates Research Scholarship, I developed the Apall Multi-Slit Extension code for automated aperture extractions on reduced and calibrated slit spectral data.

A Spectrophotometric Survey of the Supernovae-Rich NGC 6946 Galactic Host Environments is set to be published this winter and will be my first paper as the first author.

Come find me at the upcoming American Astronomical Society 231st Meeting at poster # 251.03, thanks to support from an AAS FAMOUS Travel Grant!

The Lucke-Hodge 41 OB Star Association

Characterizing OB Star Populations in the Large Magellanic Cloud

Using Hubble WFC3 Photometry and MaiGE and IMACS multi-slit spectra from the 6.5m Magellan Telescope, I determined the effective temperatures and luminosities of ~40 OB stars within LH-41 in the LMC, working with Prof. Philip Massey at Lowell Observatory. Furthermore, I modelled mass loss rates and surface gravity using FASTWIND and developed a program to match star locations to Hubble field photometry.

Our initial Hurtzsprung Russel Diagram showed that LH-41 housed a population of massive O and B stars that were not in fact coeval!

Milky Way Open Clusters

Prof. Ana Larson and I are finding MW open cluster metallicities in order to probe the evolution of the Galactic disk.

Working on this project, I observed on-site in Victoria, BC at the Dominion Astrophysical Observatory for four nights and independently developed the Open Cluster Membership Analysis Program in Python, which was supported by my 2nd Mary Gates Research Scholarship.
